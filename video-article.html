Video Article Type

Idea was to design the best video experience for the scroll articles. Edge to edge video experience for the scroll users.

Problem Statement :

How to improve the video article page for a more better User Experience than the current version.

The Aim is to design a video article page with an great user experience.

Team :

3-4 people (including PM's, developer and Me)

Technologies Used :

Sketch, HTML, Sass, jQuery, JW Player

Role :

I have the complete ownership of the product so I can build up a better video experience while maintaining the consistency of the brand. Before Getting started with my research, I wanted a quick idea about the JW Player(It’s Working and Customization). I had a discussion with a developer about possibilities and limitation of the JW Player as before designing the product I need all the information about the possible things we can try in the product. Below are some of the points which I would like to share and discuss:

  • Share: We can add up the share button instead of adding in the post but that’s not a great idea as If we tried to add up the share into the Video itself. In that case, only the video will be shared (Not the complete post). So I think it’s good to keep the share button outside as in that case user can share the complete post along with the Video.

  • Video Customization: The video elements can be customised in the way we want by adding up the custom CSS. Had to look up deep into the JW Player documentation to know the limitations.

  • Playlist: The JW itself provides playlist addon with a stack of examples.

  • Next-Prev: While watching a video you can switch to the next and previous videos by simply hitting the next-previous buttons.

  • Full-width Video: We can also customize the height and width of the video according to the aspect ratio.

  • Elements on Video: We can add a variety of elements on the video such as Title, Description, Buttons etc.

  • Picture-in-picture Mode: While scrolling down the page users can still watch the video via a short thumbnail with an access to go back to that video or close it down.
